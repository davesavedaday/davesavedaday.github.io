Computational Evidence for the Validity of the Balloon Risk Aversion Task (BRAT)

Balloon Risk Aversion Task (BRAT) is an inverted design of the conventional BART task. In this project, we explore the computational evidence for whether the change in the task’s design has succeeded in changing the cognitive processes of the subject. We utilize hierarchal Bayesian inference and Maximum Likelihood Estimation to find a suitable cognitive model of risk aversion. We evaluate four different model’s performance through LOOIC, AIC, and BIC, and we attempt parameter recovery to validate the model design.
